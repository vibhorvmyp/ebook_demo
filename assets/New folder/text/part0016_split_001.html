Book One

Mana eva manuṣyanam karaṇam bandha mokṣayoho.

As the mind, so the person; bondage or liberation is in your own mind. (Sanskrit saying) I-2; see also II-25

Mokṣabhekṣo bandhaḥ.

Even the desire for liberation is a bondage. (Vedantic scriptures) I-15

Man me Rām, hath me kām.

In mind, Ram (God); in the hand, work.
(Hindi saying) I-16; see also II-27

Ekam sat, viprahā bahudha vadanti.

Truth is one; seers express it in many ways. (Upaniṣads) I-27

Nantaḥ-prajñām, na bahis prajñām, nobhayatah-prajñām, na prajñām-ghanam, na prajñām, nāprajñām.

Not the outside knowledge, nor inside knowledge, not knowledge itself, not ignorance (Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad) I-49

Mauna vākya Prākṛtita Parabrahma tattvam.

The Parabrahma tattvam, or unmanifested supreme principle, can only be explained by silence, not by words. (Dakṣiṇāmūrti Stothram) I-49
